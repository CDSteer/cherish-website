Digital Economy Crucible

What is Digital Economy Crucible?

Digital Economy Crucible is one of the integral streams of CHERISH-DE (Challenging Human Environments and Research Impact for a Sustainable and Healthy Digital Economy) project. CHERISH-DE is a five-year initiative funded by EPSRC that explores how to deliver digital innovations that help people relate and respond to our rapidly expanding technological world, with a focus on health and social care, resource-constrained communities, and cyber safety and security. Crucible in general is an exclusive development opportunity for aspiring junior academics to understand the wider context in which they work and to develop a broader perspective of themselves, how they might progress within their longer-term career and, in particular, build a strengthened network for work-related collaboration and peer support. Participants are challenged to become collaborative ‘outward-facing’ researchers who seek opportunities to engage and have greater impact. (NESTA, 2014) Over the life of the project, Digital Economy Crucible seeks to build and provide leadership to a vibrant UK network of at least 100 Early Career Researchers (ECRs) engaged in interdisciplinary work aligned to the Digital Economy theme. It supports cross-disciplinary research collaboration and innovation, with ECRs participating in a series of residential workshops (‘skills labs’). Digital Economy Crucible will support five cohorts of 25 ECRs each year, drawn primarily from across all national Next Stage Digital Economy Centres (see DE-Network page on the website) and also received from other UK universities. This is the first Digital Economy Crucible call for applications of the project.

Digital Economy Crucible 2016

Twenty five successful applicants will be selected to participate in three two-day intensive skills labs, which will be residential events. As a prerequisite to receiving a place on the Digital Economy Crucible programme, the participants must commit to attending all three skills labs. Location and dates are as below:
  • Skills Lab 1 – Swansea – 19th and 20th May 2016

  • Skills Lab 2 – York – 16th and 17th June 2016

  • Skills Lab 3 – London – 28th and 29th of July 2016
 As a core part of the programme, it will facilitate gathering of researchers engaged in interdisciplinary work with distinguished guest speakers from interdisciplinary areas, including digital technology, and a series of seminars, intensive skills sessions and informal discussions. Digital economy-style approach will be the overarching theme through the skills labs, and where possible, will relate to the three contexts of the project: health and social care, resource-constrained communities, and cyber safety and security.

How will Digital Economy Crucible benefit you?

Digital Economy Crucible will enable you to:
  • explore the advantage of working with researchers in other disciplines

  • understand how your research can have greater impact

  • strengthen knowledge on how you can build international research careers

  • develop a network of peers within the research community

  • introduce you to creative and innovative thinking and improve your effectiveness both within and beyond your organisation

  • develop skills in public engagement

  • discover how you can interact more effectively with external parties
 Your employers will also benefit from your new approach of thinking, strengthened skill sets, expanded network within the research community, and links with external parties.
