How to Apply?

The call for applications will be published on 22nd January 2016 and will close on 14th March 2016. Please check CHERISH-DE website for more information. Applicants will be notified of the outcome of their application via email by end of March 2016. We will select 25 applicants with up to an additional 5 on a short waiting list.

Who can apply?

Applications are invited from ECRs with one to seven years’ postdoctoral research experience or lectureship/teaching position in any discipline in the following universities:
  • University of Bath

  • University of Nottingham

  • University of York

  • Newcastle University

  • Swansea University

  • UCL (University College of London)
However, applications may also be welcomed from talented ECRs currently working in other UK universities. Applicants must be able to commit to attending the full Digital Economy Crucible residential programmes.

What costs are covered?

All residential costs, including accommodation and catering, and costs for all sessions in the three skills labs, will be provided for successful Digital Economy Crucible candidates. They will need to fund the travel expenses independently.

How will participants be selected?

Participants will be selected by a panel of CHERISH-DE Co-Investigators through a competitive process based on their written applications. In selecting participants, the panel will be looking for researchers with the following criteria:
  • work published in their field and who are eager to take advantage of cross-discipline working

  • strong interest in digital technology, and enthusiastic about harnessing its potential to meet every day human challenges

  • good awareness of the international context of the research and starting to show evidence of recognition in the community on an international scale

  • creative and innovative thinking

  • evidence of showing leadership within the research community and evidence of pushing the boundaries of their research area

  • commitment to thinking about the impact of their work and to communicating their research beyond the academy

  • some experience in identifying, exploring and developing research opportunities more broadly and across different interfaces

  • strong understanding of and commitment to CHERISH-DE’s mission
